## Matter
Anything which has mass and occupies space.
## Non Matter
Non matter refers to the thing that do not occupy space , have mass , or consist of particles. 
## Chemical Classification of Matter
Matter is classified into two - 
- Pure Substance 
	- Element 
	- Compound 
- Mixture
	- Homogeneous 
	- Heterogeneous 
### Pure Substance 
- A material containing only one type of substance 
- It can not be separated into simpler substances by physical methods.
- They are of two types -
	- Element 
		 - A pure substance containing one type of atom. Eg. Fe, Na,Cs, etc.
	- Compound 
		 - A Pure substance containing more than one kind of element which combine together in a fixed mass ratio.
		 - They can be separated into simple substance by chemical method.
		 - The property of the compound is entirely different from those of their constituent elements.
		 - Example - HCl, H20, NH3, etc

### Mixture
- A material which consist of more than one type of substance and which are mixed in any ration by weight
- The property of the mixture is the property of its compound 
- This can be separated into its substance by physical method.
- They are of two types - 
	- Homogeneous 
		- A mixture having same composition through out the mixture. Ex. Salt+Water, Sugar+Water, etc. 
	- Heterogeneous 
		- The mixture in which compounds are present non-uniformly. Ex. Water+Sand, Water+oil, Blood, etc. 
## Atom
Smallest particle of an element which may or may not have independent existence.
## Molecule
- Smallest particle of an element or compound which always have independent existence. 
- In a molecule two or more atoms are connected by chemical bond.
- The smallest particle of the substance that has all of the physical and chemical properties of that substance.
## Atomicity of Molecules
- Number of atom present in a molecule or ion.
- Ex. H<sub>2</sub>O = 3 , KMnO<sub>4</sub> = 6 
## Representation of an element
X<sub>z</sub><sup>a</sup>  
- a -- mass number (P + n)
- z -- atomic number 
- X -- symbol of an element

## Isotopes
- Atoms of the same element having the same number of proton but different mass number.
- Example:- Carbon-12, 13, and 14 
## Isobars
- Atoms having different number of proton and neutron but same number of nucleons(neutron+proton).
- Example:- K<sub>19</sub><sup>40</sup> , Ca<sub>20</sub><sup>40</sup> , and Ar<sub>18</sub><sup>40</sup> 
## Isotones
- They have different number of proton and mass numbers but the number of neutrons are same. 
- Example:- Cl<sub>17</sub><sup>37</sup> and Ca<sub>20</sub><sup>40</sup>
## Isodiaphers
- They have same difference between proton and neutrons.
